Key Findings

GDP-Health Correlation

Our analysis reveals a strong positive correlation (r=0.78) between GDP per capita and life expectancy across 189 countries.

Key Insight: Economic growth of 1% annually can lead to approximately 0.7-1.2 years increase in life expectancy over a decade.

Infant Mortality Impact

Reducing infant mortality by 10 per 1,000 live births correlates with a 2.1 year increase in overall life expectancy.

Key Insight: Countries that reduced infant mortality by >50% in the last 20 years saw life expectancy gains of 8+ years.

Nutrition & Lifestyle

Countries with higher fruit/vegetable consumption (>400g daily per capita) show 3.4 years higher average life expectancy.

Key Insight: Lifestyle factors account for approximately 30% of the variation in life expectancy between similar income countries.

Disease Control Impact

Effective infectious disease control programs can increase national life expectancy by 4-7 years within a generation.

Key Insight: Countries with comprehensive vaccination programs show 62% lower mortality from preventable diseases.
